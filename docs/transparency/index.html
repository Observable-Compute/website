Organizational Purpose

Under Article III of the Foundation's Articles of Incorporation, we are organized exclusively for charitable, educational, and scientific purposes. Our specific mandate includes:

  • (a) Empirical research on technology terminology evolution and legal-technical alignment
  • (b) Publication of reports documenting terminology emergence, definition shifts, and translation gaps across research, legal, industry, and public domains
  • (c) Maintenance of a public database tracking technology concept clustering and adoption patterns
  • (d) Educational workshops and resources for underserved rural communities on technology literacy and AI systems
  • (e) Development and dissemination of open-source research frameworks and tools

The list above reflects the purposes enumerated in the Foundation's chartered Articles. Under purposes (d) and (e), the Foundation's current program emphasis is workforce readiness, AI literacy, K-12 education research, and free technology access for rural and Tribal communities — the work documented across our open-access publications.

Our Commitment

As a nonprofit research foundation, our primary obligation is to the public interest. We operate with strict independence from commercial pressures that often dictate the trajectory of proprietary AI development.

Our research methodologies, datasets, and the Terminology Database are maintained as open resources. We believe that mitigating the risks associated with rapid technological advancement requires transparent, observable systems accessible to researchers, policymakers, and the general public.

Financial Transparency

The Foundation is funded through foundational grants and public contributions. The Foundation received its IRS determination of 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status on May 6, 2026, with exemption effective February 23, 2026 (its date of incorporation). Annual Form 990 filings will be made available in this section beginning with the Foundation's first full fiscal year.

No part of the net earnings of the Foundation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its members, directors, officers, or other private persons, except as reasonable compensation for services rendered.
